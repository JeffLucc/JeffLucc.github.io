Where it started

Childhood interests and why engineering felt like the right language.

Early curiosities

When I was younger, I spent a lot of time around hardware. I built computers, got interested in circuit board design and the etching process, and put together a wireless repeater when I was about fourteen. Seeing something physical work because of choices I made in the design felt natural to me.

  • Building and upgrading PCs instead of just using them.
  • Thinking about how circuits were laid out, not just what they powered.
  • Experimenting with networking through a home built repeater.

Why engineering felt right

Engineering appealed to me because it connects theory to real world application. I like seeing ideas land in hardware, systems, and tools that people actually use.

Electrical Engineering stood out because it is versatile. It lets me move toward computer focused work or more traditional electrical work while still staying close to the things I cared about as a kid.

Real world impact Hands on systems Room to pivot
Why Electrical Engineering and where I am heading

Connecting versatility, signal processing, and space focused goals.

Versatility that matches my interests

Electrical Engineering lets me sit in the overlap of circuits, computing, and systems. It lines up with my early interest in computers and board design, but it does not lock me into one narrow path. I can work closer to computer engineering, closer to power systems, or in the signal processing space where a lot of interesting problems live.

Signal processing and space

Signal processing turned out to be more fun than I expected. I like seeing how filters and transforms can pull structure out of messy data. At an MDA open house, I got to see work around Canadarm projects and how sensing, control, and engineering come together in space applications. That felt like my interests and career goals overlapping in a very direct way.

Long term direction

Long term, I want a wide range of engineering experience behind me, across hardware, systems, and tooling. The goal is to work directly in the space industry or on systems that support it. I want to bring together my Electrical Engineering background, my early interest in space, and the practical mindset I have developed through projects and work.

Interests and strengths

What I enjoy and how I show up on a team.

What I enjoy doing

I am very in tune with the computer building space and the business that surrounds it. I pay attention to hardware specs, performance, and how parts fit together as a full build.

Outside of that, I enjoy going to the gym. It is satisfying to see numbers move in the right direction and feel the progress in a tangible way. I also play video games in my downtime which keeps me connected to the user side of the hardware I care about.

How I contribute to a team

Communication is one of my biggest strengths. I like being the person who can bring a team together, keep conversations open, and make sure everyone understands what we are doing.

I am aware that I may not always be the strongest technical person in the room, and I am fine with that. A good team is not made only of star players. You also need people who can connect ideas, keep things grounded, and make sure everyone is pulling in the same direction.

Values, personality, and learning

How I try to act, how people see me, and how I think about growth.

What matters to me

Honesty and integrity are important to me. I care about being able to take accountability when something goes wrong, to be upfront about it, and to treat it as a chance to learn rather than something to hide.

I would rather be clear about what I know and what I do not know than overpromise. That mindset carries into how I work, communicate, and ask for help.

Honesty Integrity Ownership

How people see me and how I learn

Friends would probably describe me as talkative, quirky, and a bit aloof at times. I take that as a sign that I bring some energy into conversations while also thinking in my own way about problems.

My view on learning: learning has no limit, and most learning is done before you start, when you decide to open yourself up to learn.

That shows up in how I approach new tools, projects, and roles. Once I decide to lean into something, I am ready to adapt, listen, and build from there.
