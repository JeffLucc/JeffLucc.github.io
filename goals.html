// Direction

I am currently employed in a software focused role, but my long term direction is to build on my Electrical Engineering and astrophysics background. I want to work on systems where sensors, power, and computation come together, with a particular interest in applications that support space exploration and space based infrastructure.

1. Goals and Work Portfolio

How my projects and experience guide the type of electrical engineer I want to become.

Short term goals

In the next one to two years, I want to tighten the connection between the work I do day to day and the core of Electrical Engineering.

  • Deepen my understanding of sensing and instrumentation by refining projects like my automatic plant watering system and infrared heart rate monitor, with better calibration, enclosure design, and robustness.
  • Strengthen my signal processing skills by extending my audio filter designs into more advanced filtering and real time implementations using tools such as MATLAB and embedded platforms.
  • Build on my ETAP power flow analysis work by taking on more complex transmission scenarios and tying the results to real world reliability and stability concerns.
  • Continue to use software as a tool to support electrical work, for example by automating analysis, simulations, or report generation for hardware and power focused projects.

Long term goals

Longer term, I want my career to sit at the intersection of Electrical Engineering, software, and space.

  • Work on systems that involve sensing, power management, and communication in challenging environments, such as remote or off planet applications.
  • Leverage my astrophysics background to better understand the constraints and opportunities of space based systems, and apply my engineering skillset to missions, infrastructure, or tools that support space science.
  • Grow into roles where I can design and validate hardware and simulations, and build the software tooling that other engineers use to understand system behavior and make decisions.
  • Contribute to teams that value clear documentation, thoughtful trade offs, and a strong link between theoretical models and measured performance.

Work portfolio highlights

These projects represent the kind of work I enjoy most: using sensors to understand the physical world, shaping signals into something usable, and analyzing how power moves through a system.

Automatic plant watering and drainage system

Designed a control system that monitors soil humidity and automatically manages watering and drainage. This combined sensor selection, analog interfacing, and control logic, and gave me hands on experience with reliability issues in a real environment.

Infrared and phototransistor heart rate monitor

Built a non invasive heart rate monitor using an infrared LED and phototransistor pair, then amplified and filtered the signal to extract a clean heart rate waveform. This sharpened my skills in analog front end design and noise reduction.

Sound filtering and frequency shaping

Designed filter circuits and signal processing chains to remove unwanted noise and emphasize desired frequency bands. This showed how design choices in filters directly affect which information is preserved or lost in a signal.

Power flow analysis with ETAP

Performed power flow and contingency analysis on transmission systems using ETAP to evaluate system loading, volatility, and stability under different operating conditions.
