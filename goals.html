Goals and Work Portfolio

A snapshot of the projects that shaped my interests in sensing, signal processing, power systems, and how I think about problems beyond pure engineering.

Portfolio highlights

These projects show how I like to work: measure the real world, shape signals into something useful, and study how power and systems behave under load.

Automatic plant watering and drainage system

Designed a control system that monitors soil humidity and automatically manages watering and drainage. This combined sensor selection, analog interfacing, and control logic, and gave me hands on experience with reliability issues in a real environment.

Infrared and phototransistor heart rate monitor

Built a non invasive heart rate monitor using an infrared LED and phototransistor pair, then amplified and filtered the signal to extract a clean heart rate waveform. This sharpened my skills in analog front end design and noise reduction.

Sound filtering and frequency shaping

Designed filter circuits and signal processing chains to remove unwanted noise and emphasize desired frequency bands. This showed how design choices in filters directly affect which information is preserved or lost in a signal.

Power flow analysis with ETAP

Performed power flow and contingency analysis on transmission systems using ETAP to evaluate system loading, volatility, and stability under different operating conditions.

Cross disciplinary projects

Where business and systems thinking meet engineering work.

Business and systems view

Courses like Business Essentials for Technology Entrepreneurs I and II gave me practice looking at technology from the outside in: markets, customers, and strategy, not just circuits and code.

  • Tom.com and iQmetrix SWOT case study: Analyzed both companies in terms of strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats, tying technology choices to revenue models, partnerships, and market positioning.
  • Business Essentials for Technology Entrepreneurs: Worked with ideas like value propositions, customer segments, and simple financial thinking, and practiced explaining technical work in clear business language.
  • Remote secure SSH tunnel and Plex automation: Set up a remote secure SSH tunnel to a Linux PC server that automatically indexes and downloads media, then serves it through a Plex snap package. This pulled together networking, security, automation, and user experience into one system.
